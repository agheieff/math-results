# Prior-art scope

Arnav Krishnan's 2026 correction proves the uniform upper bound
\(Z(P(n,3))\le8\) for \(n\ge9\), gives the corrected finite table through \(n=20\), and states
\(Z(P(n,3))=8\) for every \(n\ge13\) as a conjecture. It explicitly identifies uniform
exclusion of seven-vertex forcing sets as the missing lower-bound step.

Primary source:

- Arnav Krishnan, “A correction to the Zero Forcing Number of the Generalized Petersen Graphs
  \(P(n,3)\),” [arXiv:2607.19412v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19412v1), 13 July 2026.

The earlier claimed all-\(n\) theorem is false at \(n=12\):

- S. Rashidi, N. Shajareh Poursalavati, and M. Tavakkoli, “Computing the zero forcing number
  for generalized Petersen graphs,” *J. Algebra Comb. Discrete Struct. Appl.* 7 (2020),
  183–193, [doi:10.13069/jacodesmath.729465](https://doi.org/10.13069/jacodesmath.729465).

This package makes no literature-wide novelty claim for infinite-graph zero forcing. Its role
is to freeze a self-contained structural lemma for the finite conjecture.
