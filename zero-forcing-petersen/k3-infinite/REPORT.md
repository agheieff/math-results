# Research status

## Proved here

- The finite-seed, finitary zero forcing number of the bi-infinite strip is exactly eight.
- Its adjacency-kernel solution space is exactly eight-dimensional, with an explicit rational
  recurrence and evaluation rows.
- Eight consecutive outer vertices propagate in both directions by a translated local force
  sequence.
- \(P(n-3,3)\) is a minor of \(P(n,3)\) for every \(n\ge10\).

## Not proved here

The finite conjecture

\[
Z(P(n,3))=8\qquad(n\ge13)
\]

remains open in this lane. A finite cyclic quotient can gain wrap-around interactions absent
from the bi-infinite graph: seven consecutive outer vertices force \(P(11,3)\) and
\(P(12,3)\), while they do not force the strip. Therefore the strip theorem alone is not a
valid transfer argument.

Existing exact local packages certify the finite conjecture through \(n=33\), and their
seven-set closures have maximum size 16 from \(n=21\) onward. That repeated finite behavior is
motivation for a bounded-locality theorem, not a proof of one.
