# Red-team notes

- The infinite convention is stated explicitly: finite seed, and a finite derivation for each
  vertex. No cardinality argument on infinite closures is used.
- The lower bound uses an actual nonzero adjacency-kernel solution vanishing on any prescribed
  set of at most seven vertices. Every force preserves the property “known coordinates are
  zero.”
- The recurrence is reversible at both ends, so the eight initial outer coordinates give
  exactly, not merely at most, eight dimensions.
- The upper proof expands both ends of an eight-outer-vertex block by displayed legal forces.
  Translation and monotonicity give a finite derivation to every fixed index.
- The minor lemma does not imply ordinary zero-forcing monotonicity and is not presented as a
  proof of the finite conjecture.
- Finite cyclic wrap-around is a real obstruction to naive transfer, witnessed at \(n=11,12\).
