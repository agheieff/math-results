# Prior art and provenance

- Ghaith Hiary's
  [sample-data page](https://people.math.osu.edu/hiary.1/amortized.html) supplies the
  \(T=10^{25}\) zero and derivative tables. It defines the derivative column as
  \(Z'(T+t_j)\), reports typical zero-ordinate error \(5\cdot10^{-12}\), derivative
  error up to \(10^{-5}\), and cross-checks against some Odlyzko data.
- Andrew Odlyzko's
  [zeta-zero tables](https://www-users.cse.umn.edu/~odlyzko/zeta_tables/) supplied
  the earlier low-height sample used in the RH notes; that scan found no four-point
  gap.
- The local precursor is
  `/home/agheieff/Arcadia/RH/4/proofs/nonzero-runs.md`. It formulated the forbidden
  \(+,-,+,-\) pattern and reduced it to a Gram-count and parity question.

No claim is made that the source tables are rigorous interval-certified zero counts.
