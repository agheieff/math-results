# Result

Let

\[
\zeta\!\left(\tfrac12+it\right)=e^{-i\theta(t)}Z(t),
\qquad Z(t)\in\mathbb R.
\]

In Hiary's \(T=10^{25}\) sample, rows 218950 and 218951 are the consecutive listed
critical-line zero ordinates

\[
\begin{aligned}
\gamma_-&=10^{25}+24742.820054547281038548,\\
\gamma_+&=10^{25}+24743.226032454997212501.
\end{aligned}
\]

High-precision evaluation of the Riemann--Siegel theta expansion gives

\[
\begin{aligned}
\theta(\gamma_-)/\pi
 &=87100328229866724999398818.613436964611696\ldots,\\
\theta(\gamma_+)/\pi
 &=87100328229866724999398822.214131257026160\ldots.
\end{aligned}
\]

The open interval therefore contains exactly the four Gram indices

\[
87100328229866724999398819,\ldots,87100328229866724999398822.
\]

The table gives \(Z'(\gamma_-)=-46.586124324576864808\). Hence \(Z\) is negative
immediately to the right of the simple listed zero. At a Gram point \(g_n\),
\(\operatorname{Re}\zeta(1/2+ig_n)=(-1)^nZ(g_n)\). The first index is odd, so the
four signs are \(+,-,+,-\).

The full ten-million-row replay finds 56 gaps containing four Gram points: 30 have
the forbidden orientation \(+,-,+,-\), and 26 have the opposite orientation.
No listed gap contains five Gram points.

## Scope

This refutes the finite-data conjecture as an assertion about the published sample.
It does not constitute a certified counterexample about the exact zeta function,
because the source reports typical numerical accuracies rather than interval
certificates. The endpoint theta margins, \(0.3865\ldots\) and \(0.2141\ldots\), and
the derivative magnitude make the classification extremely insensitive to the
reported errors.
