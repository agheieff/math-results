# Prior-art note

Ge and Xu define \(m_d\) as the supremal normalized measure of a measurable zero-sum-free
subset of \(S^{d-1}\) and prove

\[
  m_d\leq \frac{\lfloor(d+1)^2/2\rfloor}{d(d+1)}.
\]

At \(d=3\), this is \(m_3\leq 2/3\). Their Lemma 3.1 is the finite-configuration averaging
principle used here: if every zero-sum-free subset of a finite \(F\subset S^{d-1}\) has at most
\(M\) points, then \(m_d\leq M/|F|\).

Primary sources:

- Hong-Jun Ge and Zixiang Xu, “The sharp asymptotic density of zero-sum-free spherical sets,”
  [arXiv:2607.05099v2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05099v2), revised 7 July 2026.
- Boris Bukh, problem 11 on
  [Interesting problems that I cannot solve](https://www.borisbukh.org/problems.html).

A limited search on 23 July 2026 for an \(H_3\)-specific zero-sum-triple or independence
certificate found no directly matching published treatment. This is only a source audit and
is not a novelty claim.
