# Order-13 reduction

Let `T` be a 13-vertex tournament with no strong Seymour vertex. The main theorem of
Bai--Li--Park gives `delta+(T) >= 6`. A tournament of order 13 has 78 arcs, so its average
outdegree is 6. Hence every vertex has outdegree exactly 6.

For a vertex `v`, put `A = N+(v)` and `B = N-(v)`. Both sets have size 6. The bipartite
graph relevant to the definition has an arc `x -> z` for `x in A`, `z in B`. Hall's theorem
says that `v` is not strong Seymour exactly when some nonempty `W subset A` satisfies

```text
|N+(W) intersect B| < |W|.
```

Choose `W` inclusion-minimal. It has at least three vertices:

- If `|W| = 1`, its vertex loses to `v` and all six vertices of `B`, so it has outdegree at
  most 5.
- If `|W| = 2`, their total outdegree is at most 1 inside `W`, 8 toward `A \\ W`, and 2
  toward their at-most-one neighbour in `B`, totalling 11 rather than 12.

For every `x in W`, minimality gives
`|N+(W \\ {x}) intersect B| >= |W| - 1`. This set is contained in
`N+(W) intersect B`, whose size is at most `|W| - 1`. Consequently

```text
|N+(W) intersect B| = |W| - 1.
```

The CNF uses these variables:

- `e_i_j` (`i < j`): the orientation of tournament pair `{i,j}`;
- `w_v_x`: membership of `x` in the minimal Hall witness for `v`;
- `q_v_z`: membership of `z` in `N+(W) intersect N-(v)`;
- `h_v_x_z`: the conjunction `w_v_x and (z -> v) and (x -> z)`;
- sequential-counter auxiliaries generated by PySAT.

The clauses enforce outdegree 6, `W subset N+(v)`, the exact equivalence between `q` and
the disjunction of the corresponding `h` variables, `|W| >= 3`, and `|Q| = |W| - 1`.
They do so for all 13 vertices. Finally, vertex 0 is fixed to dominate vertices 1 through 6.
This loses no generality because any regular tournament can be relabelled that way.

Thus a CNF model is exactly a relabelled order-13 counterexample equipped with one minimal
Hall witness per vertex, and every counterexample yields a CNF model. The checked UNSAT
certificate therefore proves that no such tournament exists.
