# Prior-art boundary

The 13-vertex statement is known; this lane records a transparent local proof
and rejects an overstrong matching reduction. It is not a new resolution of
the general \(\alpha=2\) case.

- M. Chudnovsky and P. Seymour, *Packing seagulls*, Combinatorica 32 (2012),
  251--282, DOI
  [10.1007/s00493-012-2594-2](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00493-012-2594-2).
  Their main theorem is the exact four-condition characterization used in
  Section 3 of `PROOF.md`.
- M. D. Plummer, M. Stiebitz, and B. Toft, *On a special case of Hadwiger's
  conjecture*, Discuss. Math. Graph Theory 23 (2003), 333--363, DOI
  [10.7151/dmgt.1206](https://doi.org/10.7151/dmgt.1206). This is the source
  for the standard minimal-counterexample properties in the
  independence-two setting.
- D. Carter, *Hadwiger's Conjecture with Certain Forbidden Induced
  Subgraphs*, Experimental Mathematics (online 2024), DOI
  [10.1080/10586458.2024.2428958](https://doi.org/10.1080/10586458.2024.2428958).
  Carter's computer-assisted \(K_8\)-unavoidability result, combined with the
  standard seagull bound, implies more broadly that every graph with
  independence number two on at most 30 vertices satisfies Hadwiger's
  conjecture.

The unresolved high-prestige target remains Hadwiger's conjecture for all
graphs with independence number two. The present lane only freezes its first
possible \(\chi=7\), \(2\chi-1=13\) base case.
