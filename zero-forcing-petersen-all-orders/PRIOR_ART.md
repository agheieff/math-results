# Prior art checked on 23 July 2026

1. S. Rashidi, N. Shajareh Poursalavati, and M. Tavakkoli, “Computing the zero forcing number
   for generalized Petersen graphs,” *J. Algebra Comb. Discrete Struct. Appl.* 7 (2020),
   183–193, [doi:10.13069/jacodesmath.729465](https://doi.org/10.13069/jacodesmath.729465).
   The paper claimed the all-orders result, but its lower-bound proof has a gap.
2. Arnav Krishnan, “A correction to the Zero Forcing Number of the Generalized Petersen Graphs
   \(P(n,3)\),” [arXiv:2607.19412v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19412v1), 13 July 2026. It
   reports \(Z(P(12,3))=7\), verifies \(Z(P(n,3))=8\) through \(n=20\), and leaves the
   statement for every \(n\ge13\) as a conjecture.
3. F. Barioli et al., “Parameters Related to Tree-Width, Zero Forcing, and Maximum Nullity of
   a Graph,” *J. Graph Theory* 72 (2013), 146–177,
   [doi:10.1002/jgt.21637](https://doi.org/10.1002/jgt.21637). Its parameter relations imply
   the standard lower bound \(\operatorname{pw}(G)\le Z(G)\); `THEOREM.md` also gives a direct
   forcing-order proof.
4. N. G. Kinnersley, “The vertex separation number of a graph equals its path-width,”
   *Information Processing Letters* 42 (1992), 345–350,
   [doi:10.1016/0020-0190(92)90234-M](https://doi.org/10.1016/0020-0190(92)90234-M).

A targeted search found no source proving the corrected all-orders conjecture.
